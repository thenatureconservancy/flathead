Past: Historical Ecosystems

LANDFIRE’s Biophysical Settings (BpSs)


BpS models represent the vegetation system that may have been dominant on the landscape prior to Euro-American settlement. These models are based on a combination of the current environment and an approximation of the historical disturbance regime. Read more.

Check out this 3-minute tutorial for a quick refresher on the basics of Biophysical Settings Models.

Use this page as a starting point to explore the most prevalent BpSs in the Flathead National Forest. In addition to the charts and maps, the historical disturbance data can be directly accessed using this site.

Summary

  • The Rocky Mountain Subalpine Mesic-Wet Spruce-Fir Forest and Woodland was the most dominant ecosystem historically, covering ~42% of the area.
  • The top ten most prevalent BpSs covered ~90% of the landscape. LANDFIRE mapped a total of 56 types, though most were minimally mapped (sometimes just a few pixels).
  • LANDFIRE models indicate there would have been ~ 90k acres of disturbance annually, with ~24k acres being categorized as wind/weather/stress.
